Mother Teresa

The woman who served the whole world

Mary Teresa Bojaxhiu MC; 26 August 1910 – 5 September 1997), better known as Mother Teresa,[pron 1] was an Albanian-Indian Catholic nun and the founder of the Missionaries of Charity. She was born Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu[pron 2] in Skopje, part of the Ottoman Empire at the time.[b] At the age of 18, she moved to Ireland and then to India, where she lived most of her life. On 4 September 2016, she was canonised by the Catholic Church as Saint Teresa of Calcutta. The anniversary of her death, 5 September, is her feast day.

Here's a time line of Mother Teresa:

  • 1910 - August 26: Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu is born in Skopje, Ottoman Empire (now North Macedonia) to Albanian parents.
  • 1928 -Agnes joins the Sisters of Loreto in Dublin, Ireland, and takes the name Sister Mary Teresa.
  • 1929 -Arrives in India and begins her novitiate in Darjeeling, West Bengal.
  • 1931 - Makes her first profession of vows, becoming Sister Teresa..
  • 1937 -Takes her final vows as a nun and is assigned to teach at St. Mary's High School in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India.
  • 1946 -September 10: Experiences her "call within a call" on a train journey to Darjeeling, feeling compelled to serve the poor.
  • 1948 - Receives permission from the Vatican to leave the Sisters of Loreto and start a new order, the Missionaries of Charity.
  • 1950 - The Missionaries of Charity is established, with a mission to care for "the hungry, the naked, the homeless, the crippled, the blind, the lepers, all those people who feel unwanted, unloved, uncared for throughout society, people that have become a burden to the society and are shunned by everyone."
  • 1952 - Mother Teresa opens Nirmal Hriday (Pure Heart) Home for Dying Destitutes in Kolkata, providing care to the terminally ill.
  • 1955 - The congregation's first orphanage, Shishu Bhavan, is founded.
  • 1962 - The Missionaries of Charity receive official recognition from the Archdiocese of Calcutta.
  • 1971 - Receives the first Pope John XXIII Peace Prize in recognition of her humanitarian efforts.
  • 1979 - Receives the Nobel Peace Prize for her selfless work in alleviating the suffering of the poor and destitute.
  • 1982 - Survives an assassination attempt in Berlin.
  • 1991 - Mother Teresa resigns as the head of the Missionaries of Charity due to declining health but continues to serve actively.
  • 1997 - September 5: Mother Teresa passes away in Kolkata at the age of 87.
  • 2003 - Beatified as "Blessed Teresa of Calcutta" by Pope John Paul II.
  • 2016 - Canonized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta by Pope Francis.

"Mother Teresa's life was marked by unwavering dedication and compassion, making a profound impact on the lives of countless individuals in need. Her legacy continues to inspire people worldwide to serve humanity with love and selflessness."
